\chapter{Methodology}
\label{ch:methodology}

This chapter describes the research methodology used in this study.

\section{Research Design}

This research follows a design science methodology, developing and evaluating artifacts for policy formalization.

\subsection{Research Phases}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Phase 1: Data Collection} --- Gather policy documents and create gold standard
    \item \textbf{Phase 2: LLM Evaluation} --- Compare models for rule identification
    \item \textbf{Phase 3: Formalization} --- Develop FOL representation approach
    \item \textbf{Phase 4: Translation} --- Convert FOL to SHACL shapes
    \item \textbf{Phase 5: Validation} --- Test with sample data
\end{enumerate}

\section{Data Collection}

\subsection{Policy Documents}

We collected five AIT Policies and Procedures (P\&P) documents:

\begin{itemize}
    \item FB-6-1-1: Credit Policy
    \item FB-6-1-2: Accommodation Policy
    \item PM-4-1: Grievance Policy
    \item PM-4-2: Ethics Policy
    \item Student Handbook
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Gold Standard Creation}

Policy rules were manually extracted and classified:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item PDF text extraction using PyMuPDF
    \item Sentence segmentation
    \item Manual identification of deontic statements
    \item Classification as obligation/permission/prohibition
\end{enumerate}

Total: 97 policy rules identified across 492 candidate sentences.

\section{Formalization Approach}

\subsection{First-Order Logic with Deontic Operators}

We extend FOL with three deontic operators:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{O($\phi$)}: Obligation --- $\phi$ must hold
    \item \textbf{P($\phi$)}: Permission --- $\phi$ may hold
    \item \textbf{F($\phi$)}: Prohibition --- $\phi$ is forbidden
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Formalization Process}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Identify deontic marker (must, may, cannot, etc.)
    \item Extract subject, condition, and action
    \item Construct FOL formula with appropriate operator
    \item Expand to full first-order representation
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Example Formalization}

\textbf{Input}: ``Students must pay fees before registration''

\textbf{Output}:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Deontic formula: \texttt{O(pay(s, fees))}
    \item FOL expansion: $\forall s. Student(s) \rightarrow Before(pay(s, fees), register(s))$
\end{itemize}

\section{SHACL Translation}

\subsection{Translation Rules}

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{FOL to SHACL Mapping}
\begin{tabular}{|l|l|l|}
\hline
\textbf{FOL} & \textbf{SHACL} & \textbf{Severity} \\
\hline
Obligation O($\phi$) & sh:property + sh:minCount & sh:Violation \\
Permission P($\phi$) & sh:property (optional) & sh:Info \\
Prohibition F($\phi$) & sh:property + sh:maxCount 0 & sh:Violation \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\subsection{Output Format}

SHACL shapes in Turtle format with:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Node shapes for entity classes
    \item Property constraints
    \item Custom deontic type annotations
    \item Severity levels based on rule type
\end{itemize}

\section{Validation Approach}

\subsection{Syntactic Validation}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Parse SHACL shapes with rdflib
    \item Verify triple count and structure
    \item Check for Turtle syntax errors
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Semantic Validation}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Create sample RDF data
    \item Run SHACL validation with pyshacl
    \item Verify expected conformance results
\end{itemize}
