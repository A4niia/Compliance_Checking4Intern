\chapter{Introduction}

\section{Background and Motivation}

Organizations worldwide rely on natural language policies to govern operations, ensure compliance, and maintain standards. Academic institutions such as the Asian Institute of Technology (AIT) maintain extensive policy documents covering student conduct, financial obligations, academic integrity, and administrative procedures. However, a significant gap exists between these human-readable policies and automated systems that could enforce or monitor compliance.

The challenge of policy compliance is multifaceted:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Policy documents are written in natural language with inherent ambiguity
    \item Manual compliance checking is time-consuming and error-prone
    \item Policy violations may go undetected until significant consequences arise
    \item Updates to policies require manual propagation to all enforcement systems
\end{itemize}

Semantic Web technologies, particularly the Shapes Constraint Language (SHACL), provide a promising approach to bridge this gap. SHACL enables the formal specification of constraints on RDF data, allowing automated validation against defined rules. However, transforming natural language policies into SHACL shapes requires a systematic methodology that preserves the semantic intent of the original policy.

\section{Problem Statement}

The primary challenge addressed by this research is the lack of a rigorous, validated methodology for transforming natural language academic policies into machine-executable formal knowledge. Existing approaches suffer from:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Selection Bias}: Researchers often choose policies that are easy to formalize, without systematic analysis of what determines formalizability
    \item \textbf{Missing Validation}: The correctness of transformations is rarely formally verified
    \item \textbf{Limited Expressiveness Analysis}: No clear framework exists for determining which policies can be represented in standard formalisms
    \item \textbf{Incomplete Documentation}: The translation process from policy to formal representation lacks reproducibility
\end{enumerate}

\section{Research Questions}

This thesis addresses the following research questions:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{RQ1: Pattern Discovery}
    
    What linguistic and structural patterns in AIT academic policies can be formally represented in First-Order Logic and SHACL, and what patterns exceed the expressiveness of these formalisms?
    
    \item \textbf{RQ2: Formal Verification}
    
    How can we systematically verify that SHACL shapes correctly implement the intended policy semantics using automated equivalence checking?
    
    \item \textbf{RQ3: Evaluation}
    
    What is the overall formalization coverage, validation accuracy, and detection precision of the proposed methodology when applied to AIT policies?
\end{enumerate}

\section{Research Objectives}

The specific objectives of this research are:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item To construct a corpus of policy rules from AIT official documents (target: 80-100 rules)
    \item To develop an annotation scheme for classifying policy rules by linguistic complexity and formalizability
    \item To identify patterns that determine successful FOL/SHACL formalization
    \item To implement a multi-stage validation framework for policy translation
    \item To evaluate the methodology using real policy data with quantitative metrics
\end{enumerate}

\section{Scope and Limitations}

\subsection{In Scope}
\begin{itemize}
    \item AIT Policies and Procedures documents (5 documents)
    \item Student-related policies (financial, academic, accommodation)
    \item First-Order Logic as the intermediate formal representation
    \item SHACL Core Constraint Language components
    \item Single-language policies (English)
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Out of Scope}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Natural Language Processing automation (manual translation focus)
    \item Multi-institutional policy analysis
    \item Real-time policy change management
    \item Temporal logic extensions beyond simple deadlines
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Known Limitations}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Single annotator validation (with advisor sampling)
    \item SHACL2FOL tool may not support sh:sparql constraints
    \item Limited to policies expressible in FOL fragment
\end{itemize}
