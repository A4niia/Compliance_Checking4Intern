% Abstract
\begin{abstract}
This thesis presents an automated system for extracting and formalizing policy rules from natural language documents. We evaluate five large language models (LLMs) for rule identification, achieving 99\% accuracy with Mistral 7B. We demonstrate that First-Order Logic (FOL) with deontic operators is sufficient for policy formalization, contradicting assumptions about the need for higher-order logic. Our pipeline translates 96 policy rules into 1309 SHACL triples for RDF-based compliance validation. Key contributions include: (1) a systematic evaluation of LLMs for policy classification, (2) evidence that FOL+deontic operators suffice for institutional policies, and (3) an end-to-end pipeline from natural language to executable validation constraints.

\textbf{Keywords:} Policy formalization, First-Order Logic, Deontic logic, Large Language Models, SHACL, Compliance validation
\end{abstract}
